SOCIAL NETWORKING

Social Networking

social networking is an online platform that people use to build social networks or social relationships with other people who share similar personal or career interests. The social network is distributed across various computer networks.

Social Networking apps include but are not limited to; Facebook, Snapchat, Instagram, Tumbler, WeChat, ect.

Marketers use social networking for increasing brand recognition. Since it makes a company more accessible to new customers and more recognizable for existing customers, social networking helps promote a brand’s voice and content.

Social Networking Benefits

Social networking benefits a lot of people in spreading awareness to something, whether that be your brand or your views on the world, you can allow more people to see what you want them to see than ever before, an example of this would be companies utilizing social media to help promote their product, which in turn brings them more sales and profits to their business.

Who Started Social Networking

Mark Zuckerberg is one of the most influential social networking creators, he is the founder of Facebook one of the most popular social media platforms to date. Facebook would set the grounds for the modern use of social networks.

How social Networking affects culture

Social Networking has allowed billions all across the world to connect at the click of a button, with social networking people can also express their personality in exchange for likes and reposts, this has also allowed for a huge marketing opportunity for business and companies alike, were people utilize ads to help promote their product or service.

Four Facts about Social Networking

There are 3.4 billion active social media users

People on average spend 142 minutes a day on social media

Facebook adds 500,000 million users a day

91% of all retail brands use social media

History of Social Networking

It’s conception would begin with BBS (bulletin board system) but that was mostly used for illicit or illegal practices in the late 70s. The next incarnation would also set the course for instant messaging, and chat rooms would see a rise with Internet Relay Chat and ICQ. Profiling on sites would soon follow with early dating websites, online accounts, and friending people; the most popular of these being MySpace starting in 2003. MySpace would explode with over 75.9 million users at its peak in 2008. Facebook would be another modern social media that can be seen today, originally starting as a Harvard-only network in 2004. It would then expand to other schools, then businesses, until eventually being accessible to everyone. Youtube would also be launched the next year and soon become the biggest video sharing platform on the web. MMOs (Massive-Multiplayer Online games) would also play a huge part of its own in online interaction, as well as a gaming genre. World of Warcraft, released in 2004, was one of the first most profitable MMOs, and is still releasing new content with Blizzard’s latest expansion, “Battle for Azeroth” on August 14th, 2018. Reddit would hit the internet at the same time as Youtube in 2005, allowing users to use the same system as previous sites, such as Digg, to upvote or downvote popular content. From there, social media still used today would continue to spawn. Twitter would serve as a way for users to follow influential people, such as celebrities or their friends. Tumblr would allow people to “micro-blog” their lives by posting screenshots, videos, and liking other posts. Smartphones would then allow for constant updates by giving users access to the internet wherever they may be. Some of the most popular and well known Social Media Networks today would be Twitter, Youtube, Snapchat, and Instagram.
